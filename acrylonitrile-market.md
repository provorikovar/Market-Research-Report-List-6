<p><p>The Acrylonitrile market is primarily segmented into two production methods: the Propylene Method and the Propane Method. The Propylene Method involves the reaction of propylene with ammonia and air, producing acrylonitrile efficiently and predominantly. In contrast, the Propane Method utilizes propane as a feedstock, undergoing dehydrogenation and subsequent reactions, offering a different approach with varying cost and environmental implications. Both methods impact production capacity, pricing strategies, and the overall market dynamics for acrylonitrile-based products and applications.</p></p>
