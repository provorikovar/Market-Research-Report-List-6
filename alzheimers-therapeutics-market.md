<p><p>The Alzheimer's therapeutics market primarily includes cholinesterase inhibitors and NMDA receptor antagonists. Donepezil, rivastigmine, and galantamine are key cholinesterase inhibitors that enhance acetylcholine levels, improving cognitive function in mild to moderate Alzheimer's. Memantine, an NMDA receptor antagonist, regulates glutamate activity to protect neurons, often used in moderate to severe cases. Together, these medications address symptoms, though they do not alter disease progression, shaping a critical segment of the market focused on improving patient quality of life.</p></p>
