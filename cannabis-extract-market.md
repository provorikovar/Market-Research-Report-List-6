<p><p>The cannabis extract market includes several extraction methods, each with distinct characteristics. Sub/Supercritical CO2 extraction uses pressurized carbon dioxide to produce pure extracts without solvents. Ethanol extraction utilizes food-grade ethanol to efficiently extract cannabinoids and terpenes. Hydrocarbon extraction employs solvents like butane, producing highly potent concentrates but requires careful post-processing. Solvent-less extraction relies on mechanical methods, such as pressing or sifting, preserving the plant's natural compounds. Other methods include water extraction and advanced techniques, catering to various consumer preferences and regulatory requirements.</p></p>
