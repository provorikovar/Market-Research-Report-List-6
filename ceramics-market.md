<p><p>The ceramics market is divided into two main types: traditional ceramics and advanced ceramics. Traditional ceramics encompass common products like pottery, bricks, and tiles, primarily made from clay and used for functional and decorative purposes. In contrast, advanced ceramics comprise high-performance materials engineered for specific applications, including electronics, aerospace, and biomedical fields. Advanced ceramics exhibit superior properties, such as enhanced strength, thermal resistance, and electrical insulation, distinguishing them from their traditional counterparts in terms of functionality and application.</p></p>
