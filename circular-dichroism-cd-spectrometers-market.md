<p><ul><li>Linearly Polarized Light Sources</li><li>Circularly Polarized Light Sources</li><li>Multiple Light Sources</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Circular Dichroism (CD) Spectrometers are classified into three main types based on the light sources used for measuring the difference in absorption of left and right circularly polarized light. Linearly Polarized Light Sources use linearly polarized light for measuring CD spectra. Circularly Polarized Light Sources use circularly polarized light for higher sensitivity and less noise. Multiple Light Sources combine both linearly and circularly polarized light sources for enhanced versatility and accuracy in measuring CD spectra. Each type offers specific advantages and is chosen based on the application requirements.</p></p>
