<p><p>Digital music distribution services operate through two main market types: indirect and direct distribution. Indirect distribution involves intermediaries, such as music aggregators or labels, that streamline the process of getting music onto platforms like Spotify or Apple Music. In contrast, direct distribution allows artists to upload their music directly to streaming services, bypassing intermediaries. This approach enables greater control over distribution and revenue but may require more effort in marketing and audience engagement. Both methods serve distinct artist needs.</p></p>
