<p><p>The disposable skin stapler market is segmented primarily into two types: conventional nails and wide nails. Conventional nails are typically thinner and designed for standard wound closure, offering reliable performance in various surgical procedures. In contrast, wide nails provide a broader fastening surface, which enhances tissue apposition and reduces the risk of tissue trauma, making them suitable for more extensive surgical applications. Both types are favored for their ease of use, efficiency, and capability to streamline suturing processes in clinical settings.</p></p>
