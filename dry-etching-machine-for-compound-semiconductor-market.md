<p><p>The Dry Etching Machine for Compound Semiconductors operates using two main techniques: physical etching and chemical etching. Physical etching utilizes high-energy particles to physically remove material from the semiconductor surface, offering precise control over the etch profile. In contrast, chemical etching relies on reactive gases that chemically react with the material, allowing for more intricate and selective patterning. Both methods are essential in fabricating advanced semiconductor devices, enabling detailed microstructures while maintaining material integrity and uniformity.</p></p>
