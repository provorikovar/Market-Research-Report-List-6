<p><p>The ethylene and propylene cracking catalysts market encompasses various types, including Catalytic Hydrocracking Processes (CHP), Catalytic Recovery Processes (CRP), Catalytic Isomerization Processes (CIP), and Modified Molecular Sieve Catalysts (MMC), among others. CHP enhances feedstock conversion through hydrogenation, while CRP focuses on maximizing olefin yields from heavier hydrocarbons. CIP involves converting alkanes into more valuable isomers, and MMC utilizes modified molecular sieves for selective reactions. Each type plays a critical role in optimizing production efficiency and improving the quality of petrochemical outputs.</p></p>
