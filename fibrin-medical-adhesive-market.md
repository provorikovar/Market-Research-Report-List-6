<p><ul><li>Water-based</li><li>Solvent-based</li><li>Solids and Hot Melt-based</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fibrin medical adhesive market comprises four main types: water-based, solvent-based, solids, and hot melt-based adhesives. Water-based adhesives are biocompatible and often used for wound closure. Solvent-based adhesives offer strong bonding but may have volatile organic compounds. Solid adhesives are typically in a film or patch form, providing ease of application. Hot melt-based adhesives are thermoplastic and can be quickly applied, offering rapid bonding in surgical settings. Each type caters to different medical applications based on performance and safety requirements.</p></p>
