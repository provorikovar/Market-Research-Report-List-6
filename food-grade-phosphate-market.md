<p><p>The food-grade phosphate market comprises several key types, each serving distinct functions in food processing. Sodium tripolyphosphate (STPP) acts as a preservative and moisture-retention agent. Sodium hexametaphosphate (SHMP) is used as a gelling agent and for emulsifying. Sodium acid pyrophosphate (SAPP) functions as a leavening agent in baked goods. Tetrasodium pyrophosphate (TSPP) serves as a texturizer. Other food-grade phosphates include various blends and derivatives, which enhance flavor, stability, and shelf life in diverse food applications.</p></p>
