<p><p>Iodinated Contrast Media (ICM) are commonly used in medical imaging procedures to improve the visibility of internal organs and structures. There are two main types of ICM: Ionic Contrast Media, which contain positively or negatively charged ions, and Non-ionic Contrast Media, which do not contain charged particles. Ionic Contrast Media are more likely to cause allergic reactions and kidney damage, while Non-ionic Contrast Media are considered safer and cause fewer adverse effects. Both types of ICM play a vital role in enhancing the quality of diagnostic imaging studies.</p></p>
