<p><p>The medical batteries market features several types, each suited for specific applications. Lithium-ion (Li-ion) batteries are preferred for their high energy density and rechargeability, making them ideal for portable medical devices. Nickel-cadmium (Ni-Cd) batteries offer reliable performance and longevity, though they face limitations due to memory effect. Nickel metal hydride (NiMH) batteries provide higher capacity and are less harmful to the environment. Alkaline-manganese batteries are commonly used for non-rechargeable devices due to their affordability and availability.</p></p>
