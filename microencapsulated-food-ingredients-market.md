<p><p>The microencapsulated food ingredients market encompasses various technologies vital for enhancing ingredient stability and functionality. Spray technologies involve atomizing liquid ingredients into fine particles, effectively encapsulating them. Emulsion technologies create stable mixtures of immiscible liquids, allowing for better nutrient delivery. Dripping technologies form microcapsules by dropping a solution into a hardening agent, resulting in controlled release properties. Other methods may include coacervation, layer-by-layer encapsulation, and more, catering to diverse application needs in the food industry.</p></p>
