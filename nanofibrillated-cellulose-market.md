<p><p>The nanofibrillated cellulose market comprises three primary types: Nanofibrillated Cellulose (NFC), which is derived from mechanical and chemical treatments of wood pulp, enhancing strength and viscosity; Nanocrystalline Cellulose (NCC), produced through acid hydrolysis, offering high rigidity and optical clarity; and Bacterial Nanocellulose, synthesized by bacteria, known for its purity and unique properties suitable for biomedical applications. Each type serves distinct applications across industries such as packaging, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals, leveraging their unique characteristics for improved product performance.</p></p>
