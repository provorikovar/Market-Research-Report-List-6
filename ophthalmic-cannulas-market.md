<p><p>The ophthalmic cannulas market features two primary types: curved and straight cannulas. Curved cannulas are designed to facilitate access to difficult-to-reach areas within the eye, improving precision during surgical procedures like cataract surgery. Straight cannulas, on the other hand, offer a direct approach, making them suitable for straightforward applications, such as fluid injection or aspiration. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing surgical outcomes by providing effective delivery of medications and ensuring optimal fluid management during ophthalmic procedures.</p></p>
