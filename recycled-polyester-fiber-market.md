<p><p>The recycled polyester fiber market is primarily divided into two types: recycled polyester filament and recycled polyester staple fiber. Recycled polyester filament is produced from melted and extruded post-consumer plastics, resulting in continuous yarns ideal for applications like textiles and home furnishings. In contrast, recycled polyester staple fiber is made from shorter strands, suitable for products such as insulation, filling materials, and non-woven fabrics. Both types contribute to sustainable practices by reducing waste and resource consumption in the textile industry.</p></p>
