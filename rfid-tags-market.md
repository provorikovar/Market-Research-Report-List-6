<p><p>RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) tags are small devices that use radio waves to automatically identify and track objects. The rise of IoT (Internet of Things) is another significant trend, as RFID tags integrate with IoT systems for enhanced data collection and analysis. Furthermore, the expansion of e-commerce is pushing retailers to adopt RFID solutions for better inventory management. <p><strong>The RFID Tags Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Active</li><li>Passive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The RFID tags market is primarily divided into two types: active and passive tags. Active RFID tags have their own power source, allowing them to broadcast signals over longer distances and store more information, making them suitable for tracking high-value assets. Passive RFID tags, on the other hand, rely on an external RFID reader for power, making them smaller, cost-effective, and ideal for applications such as inventory management and access control. Each type serves distinct market needs based on functionality and cost.</p></p>
