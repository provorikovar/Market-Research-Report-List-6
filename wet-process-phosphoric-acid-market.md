<p><ul><li>Nitric Acid Method</li><li>Hydrochloric Acid Method</li><li>Sulfuric Acid Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Wet Process Phosphoric Acid market is categorized by three main production methods: Nitric Acid, Hydrochloric Acid, and Sulfuric Acid methods. The Nitric Acid Method involves the reaction of phosphorus with nitric acid, producing phosphoric acid and nitrogen oxides. The Hydrochloric Acid Method uses hydrochloric acid to dissolve phosphate rock, generating phosphoric acid. The Sulfuric Acid Method, the most common, entails reacting phosphate rock with sulfuric acid, creating phosphoric acid and gypsum as a byproduct. Each method offers distinct benefits and applications in various industries.</p></p>
